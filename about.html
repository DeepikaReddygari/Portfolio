About Me

My journey from mechanical engineering to data science and AI

My Journey

My journey into data science began in an unexpected place-a mechanical engineering classroom at JNTU Hyderabad. While studying thermodynamics and energy systems, I discovered that the real power wasn't just in understanding how systems work, but in using data to optimize them. That realization sparked a curiosity that would reshape my career.

During my undergraduate years, I immersed myself in research at IIT Hyderabad, working on cutting-edge thermal energy storage systems. I wasn't just running experiments-I was analyzing datasets, identifying patterns in temperature variations, and using statistical methods to optimize system performance. It was here that I first realized: data tells stories that transform theory into impact.

This passion led me to publish research at the POCER2019 conference, where I presented findings on renewable energy optimization. But beyond the publications, what excited me most was the potential of data-driven solutions to address real-world challenges-from energy efficiency to sustainable technology.

Building Foundations in Industry

After graduation, I joined Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) as a Systems Engineer, where I truly learned to bridge the gap between technical expertise and business value. Over two and a half years, I worked on large-scale ETL pipelines, data quality frameworks, and analytics solutions that served Fortune 500 clients. I wasn't just writing SQL queries-I was architecting data flows that powered critical business decisions.

One of my proudest achievements was being recognized as "Star of the Month" for delivering a complex data migration project ahead of schedule while maintaining 99.8% accuracy. But more than awards, what I valued was the experience of collaborating with cross-functional teams, understanding stakeholder needs, and translating technical solutions into business outcomes.

At TCS, I also discovered my love for automation and efficiency. I built Python-based data validation tools that reduced manual QA time by 40%, and created dashboards in Tableau that gave leadership real-time insights into data quality metrics. Each project taught me that the best data solutions aren't just accurate-they're scalable, maintainable, and user-friendly.

Deepening Expertise Through Graduate Studies

While I thrived in industry, I knew there was more to learn. The rapid evolution of machine learning and AI inspired me to pursue a Master's in Data Science at George Washington University. This wasn't just about gaining knowledge-it was about pushing boundaries and exploring the frontiers of what data science could achieve.

At GWU, I dove deep into advanced topics: deep learning architectures, natural language processing, reinforcement learning, and distributed computing with Spark. But theory alone wasn't enough. As a Graduate Teaching Assistant, I had the privilege of helping fellow students grasp complex concepts in Python programming and data structures, which reinforced my own understanding and taught me the value of clear communication.

My coursework became a playground for innovation. I built an Airbnb Price Prediction system using ensemble learning techniques, achieving 89% accuracy. I developed a Financial Fraud Detection model with precision scores above 0.92. I created an E-commerce Recommendation Engine that combined collaborative filtering with deep learning embeddings. Each project wasn't just an assignment-it was an opportunity to explore how AI could solve real problems.

Research and Innovation

My passion for research continued to grow at GWU. I'm currently working on battery thermal management optimization, combining my mechanical engineering background with advanced machine learning techniques. This research, which I presented at the 2025 Battery Conference, explores how AI can improve energy storage efficiency-a critical component in the transition to sustainable energy.

Beyond formal research, I've embraced hands-on learning through diverse projects. From analyzing EV market trends with time-series forecasting to building NLP-powered sentiment analysis systems, each project has expanded my technical toolkit and deepened my understanding of how different ML techniques apply to different domains.

Leadership and Community Impact

Technical skills are only part of the equation. As Operations Lead for Google Developer Groups (GDG) at GWU, I learned the importance of building communities and fostering collaboration. I organized technical workshops, coordinated hackathons, and mentored students beginning their data science journeys. These experiences taught me that the most impactful work happens when we lift others as we climb.

Similarly, my volunteer work with IEEE reinforced the value of knowledge sharing and continuous learning. Whether presenting research findings or facilitating technical discussions, I discovered that teaching others is one of the best ways to deepen your own expertise.

Looking Forward

Today, I see myself as a Data Scientist and Machine Learning Engineer who sits at the intersection of research, engineering, and real-world impact. My journey-from thermal energy research in India to building AI systems in the U.S.-has taught me that the most valuable insights come from combining diverse perspectives.

What excites me most about data science is its transformative potential. Whether it's optimizing renewable energy systems, detecting financial fraud, personalizing user experiences, or advancing scientific research, data-driven solutions have the power to make meaningful impacts on people's lives.

I'm actively seeking opportunities where I can apply my technical expertise in machine learning, NLP, and cloud-based data systems to solve challenging problems. I thrive in environments that value innovation, collaboration, and continuous learning. My goal is to build AI systems that don't just push technical boundaries-but create meaningful value for organizations and communities.

If you're working on projects at the intersection of data science, ML/AI, and real-world impact, I'd love to connect. Let's build something meaningful together.
